How I Solve LeetCode Problems as a Beginner

🏠 Home 📝 Blog 🚀 Projects

Let’s be honest—starting LeetCode is hard. You open a problem, feel stuck, scroll through solutions, and close the tab. That used to be me.

But recently, I created a step-by-step system that helps me stay consistent and actually solve problems. Here’s how I do it

Step 1: Pick the Right Problem

  • Start with Easy problems -> yes, even if you’ve coded before
  • Follow a curated list: I use LeetCode’s 75, Neetcode, or Blind 75
  • Track what I solve in Notion or a markdown file -> consistency hack 💯

Step 2: Read, Don't Panic

  • Read the question slowly
  • Write down what it asks in my own words
  • Sketch a few examples on paper or with comments

Step 3: Brute Force First

Even if it’s not efficient, I try the first approach that comes to mind. It helps me get unstuck and build confidence.

# Example: Two Sum
for i in range(len(nums)):
    for j in range(i+1, len(nums)):
        if nums[i] + nums[j] == target:
            return [i, j]

⚙ Step 4: Refactor and Optimize

Once it works, I ask: can I make it faster? Can I use a dictionary? A set?

# Optimized
seen = {}
for i, num in enumerate(nums):
    diff = target - num
    if diff in seen:
        return [seen[diff], i]
    seen[num] = i

Step 5: Write Down What I Learned

  • What was the trick?
  • What topic was involved? (HashMap, Two Pointers...)
  • What will I do differently next time?

! Bonus: I Turn It Into Content

I write blog posts or YouTube shorts about my problem-solving process. It helps me review and build a public portfolio.

Final Thoughts

Solving problems isn’t about being smart. It’s about showing up every day—even when your brain feels slow.
