What Is the Sliding Window Technique? 🪟

The Sliding Window is a technique used to solve problems involving arrays or strings efficiently.

📦 Real-World Example:

Imagine you’re looking at 3 consecutive numbers in a list. Instead of recalculating everything from scratch…

You remove one number from the left and add one on the right.

The “window” slides forward.

⚡ Why It’s Powerful:

  • Avoids unnecessary recalculations
  • Reduces nested loops
  • Often improves time complexity from O(n²) to O(n)

🧠 When to Use It:

  • Subarrays or substrings
  • Maximum/minimum sum problems
  • Longest substring without repeating characters
Don’t restart the search. Adjust it.

💻 Example in Python:


def max_sum_subarray(nums, k):
    window_sum = sum(nums[:k])
    max_sum = window_sum

    for i in range(k, len(nums)):
        window_sum = window_sum - nums[i - k] + nums[i]
        max_sum = max(max_sum, window_sum)

    return max_sum

numbers = [2, 1, 5, 1, 3, 2]
print(max_sum_subarray(numbers, 3))  # Output: 9
